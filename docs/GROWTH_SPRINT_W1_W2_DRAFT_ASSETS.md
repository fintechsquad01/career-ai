## Why Am I Not Getting Interviews in 2026?

If you are applying consistently but hearing nothing back, the fastest fix is to diagnose three failure points: ATS readability, role-match gaps, and weak proof of impact. AISkillScore helps you identify which gap is most severe first, then gives a clear next action so you do not waste applications.

### Who This Is For

- Early-career candidates with low callback rates
- Career pivoters who get views but no interviews
- Applicants unsure whether ATS or targeting is the issue

### Common Mistakes That Kill Interview Rates

1. Sending one generic resume to every role
2. Missing role-specific keywords from the JD
3. Listing tasks instead of measurable outcomes

### Recommended Process

1. Run JD Match to identify missing role requirements
2. Optimize resume language for ATS + recruiter scanning
3. Prioritize 3 applications with strongest fit before scaling

### AISkillScore Mechanism and Evidence

- Claim: Better interview outcomes start with accurate diagnosis.
- Mechanism: AISkillScore compares your resume and target JD to expose exact fit gaps.
- Evidence: Structured fit and optimization outputs reduce guesswork and improve application quality.

### FAQ

#### Is ATS the only reason I am not getting interviews?
No. ATS is one filter, but mismatch in role targeting and weak impact proof are also major blockers. Start with a fit diagnosis before rewriting everything.

#### How fast can I improve my callback rate?
Most users can identify highest-impact fixes in one session. Improvement speed depends on application quality and role competitiveness.

Run Free AI Displacement Score, then run JD Match on your highest-priority role.

## ATS Resume Checklist for Entry-Level Candidates

If your experience is limited, ATS success comes from clarity, keyword alignment, and evidence of learning velocity. This checklist gives the fastest way to improve recruiter readability without inventing experience.

### Checklist: What Hiring Teams Need To See

1. Targeted headline with role intent
2. JD-matched skills language
3. Measurable outcomes from projects/internships
4. Clean formatting and readable section hierarchy
5. Relevant keywords used naturally, not stuffed

### Common Mistakes

- Generic summaries with no role signal
- Project bullets without outcomes
- Overdesigned layouts that break parsing
