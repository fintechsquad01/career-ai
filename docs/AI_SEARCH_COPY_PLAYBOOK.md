# AI Search Copy Playbook

Version: 1.0  
Owner: Growth + Product + Content  
Last updated: 2026-02-20  
Review cadence: Monthly

Scope: Organic SEO + AI/LLM search discoverability  
Applies to: landing, guides, compare pages, pricing, tool pages, blog, lifecycle surfaces

## 1) What This Playbook Optimizes

- Ranking for high-intent organic queries
- Citation probability in AI assistants and answer engines
- Click-through to tool runs and pricing actions
- Copy consistency with AISkillScore trust and brand constraints

## 2) Canonical GEO/SEO Writing Rules

### Required

- Start with a direct answer in 40-60 words
- Use query-like H2 headers
- Keep sections compact and scannable
- Include claim -> mechanism -> evidence where persuasive claims are made
- Include one actionable next step in every major section

### Avoid

- Long generic intros before answering intent
- Hype claims without evidence
- Unstructured walls of text
- Keyword stuffing and repeated phrasing

## 3) Page-Type Blueprints

### A) Pillar Guide

Use for: broad, high-volume intent (`/guides/...`)

Structure:

1. Direct answer summary
2. Who this is for
3. Why people fail here
4. Step-by-step playbook
5. Common mistakes
6. Tool-assisted workflow with AISkillScore
7. FAQ
8. CTA: run specific tool

### B) Comparison Page

Use for: `[competitor] alternatives`, `AISkillScore vs [competitor]`

Structure:

1. Direct answer for buyer intent
2. Side-by-side scope and pricing context
3. Capability gaps that matter to outcomes
4. Trust and quality differences
5. Best-fit recommendation by persona
6. CTA: start free analysis or pricing

### C) Role/Industry Programmatic Page

Use for: `resume optimization for [role]`, `ai displacement for [industry]`

Structure:

1. Direct answer for role/industry
2. Typical pain profile for that segment
3. Checklist of what hiring teams look for
4. AISkillScore workflow by tool sequence
5. FAQ with exact role-specific queries
6. CTA: role-matched tool

### D) FAQ/Answer Page

Use for: featured snippet and AI answer capture

Structure:

1. One question per section
2. 2-4 sentence direct answer
3. Optional short explanation list
4. "What to do next" line

## 4) Query Class Mapping

- Problem-aware: "why am I not getting interviews"
- Solution-aware: "best AI resume optimizer 2026"
- Comparison: "Jobscan alternatives", "Teal vs ..."
- Execution: "how to prepare follow-up interview answers"
- Pricing/value: "pay as you go career tools"
- Resilience/income: "earn while job searching"

## 5) Internal Linking Protocol

Each new piece should link to:

- 1 relevant tool page
- 1 related guide
- pricing page when cost/value intent exists
- 1 comparison page for commercial intent traffic

Anchor text must be descriptive and intent-aligned.

## 6) AI-Agent Friendly Formatting

- Use stable terminology (AISkillScore, tokens, Job Mission Control)
- Keep sentence subjects explicit
- Use numbered processes for procedural content
- Use compact tables for comparisons where possible
- Include summary blocks that can be quoted directly

## 7) CTR and Conversion Copy Blocks (Reusable)

### Intro Block

"If you are [persona] and struggling with [pain], this guide shows the fastest path to [outcome] with evidence-backed steps."

### Value Block

"Most tools give one score. AISkillScore gives diagnosis, reasoning, and your next action."

### CTA Block

"Start with [tool] to validate your baseline, then run [next tool] to close the highest-impact gaps."

## 8) Publish QA Gate

Before publishing, confirm:

- Query intent is answered in first paragraph
- Persona and pain are explicit
- At least one trust/proof mechanism is present
- H2 hierarchy is clean and query-like
- Internal links follow protocol
- CTA is specific and low-friction

## 9) Refresh Cadence

- 14 days: evaluate early CTR and engagement
- 30 days: adjust headings and intro if underperforming
- 90 days: refresh examples, stats, and FAQ variants
- Quarterly: update for AI search freshness requirements
